## What is Clara

CLaRa (Continuous Latent Reasoning) is an end-to-end Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework developed by Apple. The core problem it addresses is that standard RAG systems suffer from two major issues: they produce long, expensive contexts, and their retrieval and generation components are trained separately with no joint optimisation signal.

CLaRa's key contribution is unifying compression, retrieval, and generation into a single differentiable pipeline that is trained end-to-end. It does this through a three-stage training process. In Stage 1, a compressor is pretrained to compress documents into compact continuous (latent) representations, using QA-based and paraphrase-guided supervision to preserve semantic meaning rather than surface patterns. In Stage 2, the compressor is fine-tuned on instruction-following QA tasks. In Stage 3, the compressor, a differentiable top-k reranker, and the generator (an LLM) are trained jointly using a single language modelling loss — meaning gradients flow back through the whole system from the final answer.

CLaRa consistently outperforms other compression-based RAG methods across evaluated benchmarks, even at high compression rates, and manages to match or exceed uncompressed retrieval baselines. However, its performance gains are less pronounced on tasks that require reasoning across multiple documents, where the lack of explicit multi-step retrieval supervision becomes a limiting factor. This makes multi-hop reasoning is one of the potential areas for further improvement, which we would explore in this paper.
